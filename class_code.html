Google is a leading multinational technology company founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin. It began as a search engine and has grown into one of the world’s largest tech giants. Google provides products and services such as Gmail, YouTube, Google Maps, Chrome, Android, and Google Drive. Its parent company, Alphabet Inc., was created in 2015. Google is also a pioneer in artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and digital advertising. With its mission “to organize the world’s information and make it universally accessible and useful,” Google has become an essential part of daily life worldwide. YouTube is the world’s largest online video-sharing platform, launched in 2005 by Steve Chen, Chad Hurley, and Jawed Karim. In 2006, it was acquired by Google. The platform allows users to upload, watch, share, and comment on videos across a wide range of topics such as education, entertainment, news, music, gaming, and more. YouTube supports live streaming and monetization through ads, memberships, and super chats, making it a major source of income for creators. With billions of users worldwide, YouTube has become a global hub for learning, creativity, and entertainment, shaping modern digital culture significantly. Instagram is a popular social media platform launched in 2010 by Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger. It was later acquired by Facebook (now Meta) in 2012. The app allows users to share photos, videos, and stories with followers, using filters and creative tools. Features like reels, live streaming, and direct messaging make it highly interactive. Instagram is also widely used by businesses and influencers for brand promotion, advertising, and connecting with audiences. With over a billion active users worldwide, Instagram has become a powerful platform for self-expression, communication, entertainment, and digital marketing in today’s social media-driven world.
